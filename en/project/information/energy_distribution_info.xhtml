Energy Distribution Information

Energy Distribution describes the mechanical device how to heat or cool the room. It does not describe heating or cooling unit

Units:

No heating / No cooling -

Description of energy distribution system:

- No heating system or air condition is equipped

Advantages:

- No energy demand for heating and cooling

Limitations:

- User has to accept occasionally air temperatures and humidity exceeding the comfort standards

- Only in combination with operable windows

Space requirement: ++

Comfort: 0

Initial cost: ++

Maintenance: ++

Most common uses:

- Residential buildings

- Schools

Radiator -

Description of energy distribution system:

- The building is equipped with radiators for heating

Advantages:

- Silent energy distribution based on water

- Small energy distribution area needed

- Reduced energy distribution transportation costs

- Quick response heating surface

Limitations:

- Radiators are visible in the rooms

Space requirement: 0

Comfort: +

Initial cost: ++

Maintenance: ++

Controllability: +

Most common uses:

- Residential buildings

- Schools

- Offices

Floor heating (radiant floor heating) -

Description of energy distribution system:

- The rooms are equipped with a radiant floor heating system

Advantages:

- Comfortable radiant heat transfer

- Silent energy distribution

Limitations:

- Slow response of heating system

Space requirement: ++

Comfort: +

Initial cost: 0

Maintenance: ++

Controllability: -

Most common uses:

- Residential buildings

Thermal Active Component -

Description of energy distribution system:

- The building is equipped with thermal active component. Cooling loads are covered by the thermal active component up to 40 W/m2

Advantages:

- Not visible in room

- Reduction of supply air volume for mechanical ventilation

Limitations:

- Attention when used in combination with operable windows in cooling season because of condensation problems on cold surface

- No dropped ceiling possible

Space requirement: +

Comfort: +

Initial cost: 0

Maintenance: ++

Controllability: 0

Most common uses:

- Offices

Cooling Ceiling (radiant cooling slab) -

Description of energy distribution system:

- The building is equipped with a cooling ceiling. Water bearing tubes are cooling or heating the ceiling. Heat transfer is done by radiation. Cooling ceiling covers heating load up to 80 W/m2

Advantages:

- Reduction of supply air volume to the hygienic minimum results in less cooling energy demand (less dehumidification)

Limitations:

- Attention when in combination with operable windows in cooling season because of condensation problems on cold surface

Space requirement: +

Comfort: + (in cold climates)

Initial cost: 0

Maintenance: 0

Controllability: +

Most common uses:

- Offices

Displacement ventilation -

Description of energy distribution system:

- The building is equipped with a displacement ventilation. The air is ventilated into a room with a relatively low temperature difference. The inlet air velocity is relatively low to avoid non-comfortable air movement for occupants. Diffuser is placed close or into the floor

Advantages:

- Comfortable cooling at small cooling loads

Limitations:

- Maximal cooling load is limited

Space requirement: +

Comfort: + (in cold climates)

Initial cost: +

Maintenance: +

Controllability: -

Most common uses:

- Offices

Mix ventilation -

Description of energy distribution system:

- The building is equipped with mix ventilation. Air is blown into the room in a relatively high velocity to heat or cool the room. Mix ventilation can cover cooling and heating loads

Advantages:

- Well known technology around the world

Limitations:

- High air volume flow leads to relatively high air transportation energy demand

Space requirement: +

Comfort: +

Initial cost: +

Maintenance: +

Controllability: +

Most common uses:

- All types of buildings
