Air Handling Information

The air handling unit is part of the mechanical ventilation system. It prepares the air for the room ventilation. The following components may be included:

  • Heating coil (provides heat to ventilated air)
  • Cooling coil (reduces ventilated air temperature)
  • Heat recovery
  • Energy recovery
  • Recirculation unit
  • Humidification devices

Options:

Natural ventilation -

  • The rooms in the building are assumed to have natural ventilation
  • The building does not have a mechanical ventilation device
  • The amount of fresh air supply by natural ventilation depends on the occupancy density

Controlled natural ventilation with automated window opener -

  • The building is equipped with controlled automated ventilation openings
  • Fresh air supply is assumed to be ventilated into the building by using demand controlled building automation strategies. The ventilation openings are assumed to be opened when the CO2 concentration is above the threshold value of 1000 ppm

Mechanical ventilation without heat recovery -

  • The building is equipped with a mechanical ventilation device
  • The mechanical ventilation system is not equipped with a heat recovery or enthalpy exchanger

Mechanical ventilation with heat recovery -

  • The building is equipped with a mechanical ventilation device
  • The mechanical ventilation system is equipped with a heat recovery. The heat recovery uses the heat of the return air to pre-heat the supply air

Mechanical ventilation with enthalpy exchanger -

  • The building is equipped with a mechanical ventilation device
  • The mechanical ventilation system is equipped with an enthalpy exchanger (energy exchanger). The enthalpy exchanger uses the heat and humidity to pre-condition the supply air. It “recovers” heat and humidity
