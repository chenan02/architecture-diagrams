Site energy demand:

The energy delivered to the building. Considered are the loss of the energy conversion system (heating / cooling unit) and distribution in the building. With the site energy demand the costs for building operation can be calculated

Primary energy demand:

The energy demand used for building operation. It includes the loss of the energy conversion systems as well energy loss of energy distribution in the public grid, transport loss etc. Loss of the electric power plants is considered as well. The carbon dioxide emission can be calculated with this value

Heating systems:

Electric warm water heating -

Domestic warm water is heated by an electric resistant heater. System is often used as permanent flow heater without water storage

Advantages:

- Small space

Disadvantages:

- Electric resistance heating is not efficient

Gas warm water heating -

Domestic warm water is heated by a gas heater. System is often used in combination with a water storage

Advantages:

- More efficient then electric WW heating

Disadvantages:

- Storage needs space

Warm water heating with room heating -

Domestic warm water is heated by the same heating system like the room heating

Advantages:

- Only one heat source

Disadvantages:

- High cost

Solar warm water heating and electric heater -

A fraction of the domestic warm water is heated by solar collectors. Domestic warm water is heated by an electric resistant heater. System is often used as permanent flow heater without water storage

Advantages:

- Solar WW is a renewable system

- Small space

Disadvantages:

- Electrical resistance heating is not efficent

- High investment cost

Solar warm water heating and gas heater -

A fraction of the domestic warm water is heated by solar collectors. Domestic warm water is heated by a gas heater. System is often used in combination with a water storage

Advantages:

Disadvantages:
