Energy Conversion Information

The energy conversion system is the mechanical heat source or cold source (heat sink) of the building. Energy will be converted into heat or cold (thermal energy). The user can chose a heating and/or a cooling system by clicking on the icon

NOTE: The diagram on the left shows the site energy of each system in kWh/m2year

NOTE: The diagram on the right shows the primary energy of each system in kWh/m2year

Site energy demand:

The energy delivered to the building. Considered are the loss of the energy conversion system (heating / cooling unit) and distribution in the building. With the site energy demand the costs for building operation can be calculated

Primary energy demand:

The energy demand used for building operation. It includes the loss of the energy conversion systems as well energy loss of energy distribution in the public grid, transport loss etc. Loss of the electric power plants is considered as well. The carbon dioxide emission can be calculated with this value

Heating systems:

Furnace -

Air is heated by gas for room heating

Efficency: 92%

Advantages:

- Conventional system based on gas

- Relatively low investment cost

Disadvantages:

- Non-renewable fuel

- Enforced air heating is needed

Boiler -

Water is heated by gas for room heating

Efficency: 92%

Advantages:

- Conventional system based on gas

- Relatively low investment cost

Disadvantages:

- Non-renewable fuel

Condensing boiler -

Water is heated by gas for room heating. System has a higher efficiency then the conventional boiler because it uses the latent heat of the exhaust

Efficency: 98%

Advantages:

- Efficent system

Disadvantages:

- Non-renewable fuel

- Needs reduced feeding temperature (temperature of the water going into energy distribution)

Biomass boiler -

Water is heated by burning a biomass like wood locks, chips or pellets

Efficency: 85%

Advantages:

- Uses renewable fuel source

Disadvantages:

- Requires large storage space

- Can only be used when biomass fuel is available at the location

Heat pump using external air -

A heat pump is used for room heating. The external air is the primary heat source for the heat pump

Seasonal Coefficient of Performance (SCOP): calculated dynamically by tool

Advantages:

- Known system

Disadvantages:

- Only efficient in locations with mild winters

Geothermal heat pump (feed temperature 35 oC)

A heat pump is used for room heating. Ground coupled loops are providing the primary heat source for the heat pump. The feed temperature is 35 oC (to achieve a higher SCOP)

Seasonal Coefficient of Performance (SCOP): calculated dynamically by tool

Advantages:

- Efficent system

Disadvantages:

- Relatively high investment cost

- Low feed temperature can cover only low heating loads

Geothermal heat pump (feed temperature 55 oC)

A heat pump is used for room heating. Ground coupled loops are providing the primary heat source for the heat pump. The feed temperature is 55 oC (standard)

Seasonal Coefficient of Performance (SCOP): calculated dynamically by tool

Advantages:

- Efficient system for high performance residential buildings

Disadvantages:

- System works only in combination with mechanical ventilation and heat recovery

- Only for high performance buildings

Cooling systems:

Split system

Compression cooling system for smaller buildings

Advantages:

- Well known system

Disadvantages:

- Low efficency

- Needs electricty for operation

Compression chiller

Compression cooling system for all types of buildings

Advantages:

- Well known system

Disadvantages:

- Needs electricty for operation

Direct cooling

Ground coupled loops are connected to energy distribution systems for room cooling. The system is combined with a compression chiller. It reduces the operation of the chiller to reduce the energy demand

Advantages:

- Efficient in climates with a relatively low ground temperature (less than 15 oC)

Disadvantages:

- Relatively high investment cost

Free cooling

The cooling tower is directly connected to energy distribution systems for room cooling. The system is combined with a compression chiller. It reduces the operation of the chiller to reduce the energy demand

Advantages:

- Efficient in arid climates

Disadvantages:

- Cooling load coverage is relatively low
