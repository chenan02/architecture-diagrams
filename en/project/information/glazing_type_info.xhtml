Glazing Type Information

Seven typical glazing types are compared in a benchmark study. The glazing types are differing in number of panes, U-Value and Solar Heat Gain Coefficient SHGC (g-Value). The window frame is estimated with 15% of the window opening area. The U-Value of the frame is adapted to the glazing property

Single pane glazing:

  • U-Value glazing:  5.2 W/m2K
  • U-Value frame:  3 W/m2K
  • SHGC:  0.82

Double pane glazing:

  • U-Value glazing:  2.8 W/m2K
  • U-Value frame:  2 W/m2K
  • SHGC:  0.78

Double pane heat protecting glazing (with argon gas filling and low-e coating):

  • U-Value glazing:  1.2 W/m2K
  • U-Value frame:  1.2 W/m2K
  • SHGC:  0.62

Double pane sun protecting glazing (tinted):

  • U-Value glazing:  2.8 W/m2K
  • U-Value frame:  2 W/m2K
  • SHGC:  0.3

Double pane sun protecting glazing (coated and argon filled):

  • U-Value glazing:  1.6 W/m2K
  • U-Value frame:  2 W/m2K
  • SHGC:  0.32

Double pane heat protecting glazing (with argon gas filling):

  • U-Value glazing:  1.6 W/m2K
  • U-Value frame:  1.6 W/m2K
  • SHGC:  0.72

Triple pane glazing (heat and sun protecting):

  • U-Value glazing:  0.7 W/m2K
  • U-Value frame:  0.6 W/m2K
  • SHGC:  0.39

Triple pane glazing (heat protecting):

  • U-Value glazing:  0.7 W/m2K
  • U-Value frame:  0.6 W/m2K
  • SHGC:  0.55
